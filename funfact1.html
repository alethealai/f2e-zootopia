Cows: The Pooing Champions

Cows poo up to 15 times a day, which can be as much as 115 pounds of manure per day — or approximately 21 tons per year!
The large quantities are often put to good use, though – manure can be turned into fertilizer, fuel, or biogas to create electricity and heat for developing parts of the world.
Cows can also sleep standing up, but they can only dream while lying down.

Bees: The Five-Eyed Flyers

Bees have five eyes — two large compound eyes at the front, like those of a fly, and three smaller eyes on top of their heads called ocelli.
These ocelli detect changes in light, helping bees sense movement and spot potential predators approaching from above.
Their incredible vision also helps them navigate, find flowers, and return to their hives with precision.

Octopuses: The Three-Hearted Wonders

Octopuses have three hearts — one pumps blood around the body, while the other two pump it to the gills.
Oh, and their blood is blue, thanks to high levels of copper!

Snails: The True Kings of Teeth

A common garden snail has around 14,000 teeth.
Their microscopic teeth are called radula, and some species actually have over 20,000 teeth! The radula acts like a tiny scraper, helping snails grind plants and algae for food.
